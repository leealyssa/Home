Incorporate Exercise Into Your Daily Routine

Are you considering to add working out to your everyday routine? If so, you have come to the right place! On this page you will learn ways to start, gather equipment, and learn about fun ways to enjoy exercising. Choosing to exercise will be one of the best choices you will make throughout your life. It reduces your risk of chronic diseases, improves your balance and coordination, helps with weight loss, and sleeping more peacefully. Not only will your current self be more energized, but your future self will be thankful.

Ways to Start

Figure Out Why You Are Working Out

First pinpoint your goals and figure out what you want to achieve. Are you working out to lose weight? Become more fit? Stay focused? Feel energized? Once you pinpoint the reason you want to workout, keep those goals in mind. It will persuade you to work towards it and act as a motivation.

Schedule Specific Times to Exercise

Although it may be difficult to take time out of your day to exercise, try to dedicate specific times primarily for working out. For example, you could watch a video while walking on a treadmill, take a break at work to go for a stroll, or read a book you've been dying to read while on a stationary bike.

Form a Routine

Try to incorporate at least 2.5 hours of adequate aerobic activity or 1.25 hours of vigorous aerobic activity every week. If you want to see results fast and crave additional health benefits to help with weight loss, it is recommended to exercise for 5 hours every week.

Try HIIT (High-Interval-Intensity Training)

“HIIT” also known as high intensity interval training is when you execute high intensity exercises in a short amount of time and in between are low-intensity exercises which give you time to recover. This type of working out can be quite challenging however, it elevates your cardio workouts to a new level.

It’s Okay to Take Days Off

Do NOT push yourself too hard. Make sure you aren’t working out for too long or with too much intensity. Stop if you are hurting yourself, and feel nauseous or lightheaded. As well, try to incorporate sessions between each exercise to allow your body to rest and recover. If you don’t feel like working out, listen to your body. It’s okay to take a day off or maybe even two.

Write this Plan Down

Writing your fitness plan down on a piece of paper, in a notebook, or in an agenda can help keep you on track and act as a reminder incase you forget.

Monitor Your Progress

Every few months, you may need to increase the amount of time you exercise and see if you are closer to your goals. Ask yourself questions. Have you gained more stamina? Has your waist shrunk or grown? Have you gained flexability? Are you at the highest point in your life? If you have lost motivation, try working out with a peer or at a fitness centre. This can make working out more fun, and encourage you to push yourself.

Gathering Equipment

Make sure you have appropriate attire for exercising. For example, if you decide to start running, buy athletic shoes to make your journey easier. When deciding to invest in exercise equipment such as weights, you might want to head to a fitness center first to try out the equipment. Furthermore, you can use your phone or other devices such as a Fitbit to track the number of steps you take, how many calories you burn, and monitor your heart rate.

KEEP IN MIND!

  • You don’t need to exercise all at once! Exercising and staying active throughout the day can add up and result in health benefits.
  • If you are injured or have medical conditions, make sure to seek advice from your doctor to help design a fitness program to help you improve your durability, flexibility, and strength.
  • You can think outside the box and exercise in fun ways! This could be going swimming with your friends or going on a bike trail with your family.
